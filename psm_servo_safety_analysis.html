1. Executive Summary

Overall SIL rating of this safety function: SIL 1
The PSM upstream detection chain (dual ASM330LHB + Mahony AHRS + FSM) achieves SIL 3 / ASIL D. However, the output channel — transistor gate stage, servo PWM generation, and servo-signal relay — contains undetected single-point failures that limit the entire safety function to SIL 1 per IEC 61508-1:2010 §10 (weakest-link rule). Two SIL levels are lost in the output chain.
Verdict on dual synced servo signals: Two PWM outputs sourced from the same MCU timer peripheral, same power supply, and same silicon die are a common-cause failure (CCF) group, not an independent redundant channel. Under IEC 61508-6 Annex D (β-factor model), β ≥ 0.20 for same-chip digital outputs. Duplicating the signal improves signal-path robustness marginally but does not improve the dangerous failure rate and introduces a stuck-on race condition as an additional failure mode.

Three findings require immediate action before deployment: (1) transistor short circuit has no detection path, (2) relay contact weld is entirely undetected, (3) no brownout detection is configured on the iMXRT1062 — MCU behaviour during slow voltage droop is undefined.

7. Dual Synced Servo Signal Assessment

7.1 Does Two Signals Equal Redundancy?

No. Under IEC 61508-2:2010 §7.4.7 and Table 3 (architectural constraints), redundancy requires independence of components, power domains, and failure modes. Two PWM outputs from the same MCU timer peripheral share silicon die, power supply, firmware, and PCB copper layer. They are a common-cause failure (CCF) group.

Per IEC 61508-6 Annex D (β-factor model): β ≥ 0.20 for same-chip digital outputs [EST]. This means at least 20 % of failures affect both channels simultaneously. This directly caps the PFH improvement from signal duplication at 80 % of one channel — the dangerous failure rate of the pair is dominated by the correlated fraction, not the independent fraction. The architecture is 1oo1, not 1oo2.

7.4 Requirements to Improve to SIL 2

The following changes are necessary (not optional) to raise the output channel from SIL 1 to SIL 2:

  1. Independent hardware cut-off path (NC relay or second transistor in series) driven from a separate GPIO, independent of servo PWM lines
  2. Transistor collector/drain voltage feedback to MCU ADC or comparator
  3. Brownout detection enabled on iMXRT1062 or external BOD IC
  4. Normally-closed relay in load path (fail-safe by de-energisation)
  5. Servo PWM signals routed to different FlexPWM sub-modules (reduces CCF β)

8. Recommendations

#PriorityRecommendationRationale
1MUSTAdd a second independent hardware cut-off (NC relay or SSR) in series with servo signal lines, driven by a separate GPIOCloses H-02 / F-03: transistor short is currently a single undetected dangerous failure. Required for SIL 2 per IEC 61508 Table 3.
2MUSTWire transistor collector/drain to MCU ADC or comparator. Assert FAULT_INTEGRITY if collector HIGH when SAFE_OUT_PIN LOW.Closes F-03 detection gap. Analogous to the existing SAFE_MON_PIN readback on pin 3.
3MUSTEnable brownout detection: configure iMXRT1062 DCDC_REG3[TRG] threshold or add external BOD ICCloses H-10 / F-16. No BOD is the highest-likelihood dangerous failure path at C (Occasional) likelihood.
4MUSTAdd 10 kΩ external pull-down resistor on SAFE_OUT_PIN net to ensure transistor turns off during MCU reset, GPIO float, and startupCloses H-11. Should be on the transistor base/gate net, not on pin 3 (SAFE_MON).
5SHOULDReplace servo-signal relay with or supplement with a normally-closed (NC) relay in load path, energised to open (fail-safe by de-energisation)Reverses F-12 failure mode direction: contact weld now means load de-energised (safe) rather than continuously powered (dangerous).
6SHOULDRoute the two servo PWM signals to different FlexPWM sub-modules on iMXRT1062 (e.g. FLEXPWM1 and FLEXPWM2)Reduces CCF β-factor for H-07 / F-17. Does not achieve true redundancy but measurably reduces correlated failure probability.
7SHOULDAdd servo position readback via analog feedback (potentiometer wiper) to a PSM analog inputDetects F-11 (seized servo) and enables closed-loop confirmation that safe position was reached within N ms of SAFE_OUT_PIN going LOW.
8SHOULDDocument system SIL constraint in safety requirements specification: "This safety function is limited to SIL 1 by the output channel architecture until items 1–4 above are implemented."Required by IEC 61508-1 §10 for traceability and to prevent design intent mismatch in future revisions.
9CONSIDERAdd an external windowed watchdog IC with independent oscillator; enable input wired in series with SAFE_OUT_PIN netCloses PSM single-WDT gap. Provides hardware-enforced time-out independent of MCU firmware or power supply state.
10CONSIDERImplement load current monitoring on relay output to detect F-12 (contacts welded) and F-13 (contacts open)Closes the relay contact detection gap for both dangerous (welded) and safe (open) failure modes.
